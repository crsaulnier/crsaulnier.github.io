Wilderness Engineering
Curriculum Development Research

From 2014 to 2017 I developed, taught, and refined a wilderness engineering class with the help of my colleagues at the MIT-SUTD Global Leadership Program. The class was created to explore alternate conceptions of authenticity in design education. After taking over 120 students on twelve wilderness expeditions we have published a variety of mixed-methods and qualitative research in 2 journal articles and 6 peer-reviewed conference publications. Before returning to consider the results of our educational experiment, I will first explain what wilderness engineering is.

A wilderness engineering experience allows students to authentically engage in a design process in which they are the designers and users of artifacts engineered for and/or in a wilderness environment.

Designing products for this unfamiliar environment forces students to think about and engage with the design process in a new and exciting way. Feedback from the natural world allows students to interact with their products at an emotional level, helping establish a deeper connection to the design process.

Living and designing in the wilderness encourages students to interpret the natural world through a scientific lens, developing an engineering science worldview. The challenges faced by each team in this environment also creates opportunities for leadership development.

Learning Objectives

Students will be able to:

  • appreciate the role of engineering in daily life;
  • interpret new problems and the natural world through the lens of engineering science;
  • think about engineering design as a circuitous process requiring creativity, resilience, boldness, agility, courage and communication;
  • confidently apply a design process to solve new problems;
  • appreciate the nuances of group dynamics, effective leadership and teamwork; and
  • have a positive experience which gets them excited about engineering.

Connection to Research

Recent work in grounded cognition has strengthened the link between physical experience and science learning1,2.

Extended wilderness experiences increase ill-structured problem solving ability3, a key competency for engineering students practicing design thinking4.

Leadership and communication skills are a focus of modern engineering education5,6. Wilderness education pedagogy supports these learning objectives, as participants in wilderness education experiences typically express long-term increased competency in leadership, teamwork, self-confidence, and communication7-9.
